Timeline of 'Modern' Games

1996

Super Mario 64

Super Mario 64 was one of the most revolutionary games of the decade as it was the start for the 3d genre of gaming. The game incorporates a hubworld, a beautiful story line, fool-proof controls, and impressive audio for the time.

1998

The Legend of Zelda: Ocarina of Time

The Legend of Zelda: Ocarina of Time was a game with innovative story-telling, a robust combat system which included “Z-Targeting”, new camera system, and Context-Sensitive Controls. All of these elements helped sell this new “type” of game, reaching 7.6 million downloads.

2011

Terraria

A revival in the 2D genre of gaming; Terraria provided gamers with a online multiplayer experience in a pixelated world. The game allows players to communicate with others, beat bosses, and adventure/upgrade gear in a unorthodox way.

2019

Control

With one of the most realistic physics engines, Control allowed players to truely immerse themselves in a new exciting adventure. The game supported DLSS, Direct X Raytracing, and many other graphics settings which enchanted the game.

2020

Call of Duty: Warzone

Call of Duty: Warzone is the biggest hit out online right now, twitch viewership ranges from 130k--->300k+. Online games like Call of Duty: Warzone, allow players to connect together, form friendships and compete against each other. The game includes a ‘Battle Royal” mode like seen in Fortnite; where players drop in, find loot, and start competing in a 150 player game.
